About The Craft

Zardozi is an intricate form of embroidery that originated in Persia and later gained popularity in India. The word "Zardozi" is derived from the Persian words "Zar," meaning gold, and "Dozi," meaning embroidery. It is characterized by the use of metallic threads, usually gold or silver, along with embellishments like beads, sequins, and stones, to create stunning designs on fabric. While Zardozi embroidery has ancient roots, it continues to be a popular and cherished art form in India and other parts of the world. It is often associated with special occasions, weddings, and festive celebrations, where the intricately embroidered garments and accessories add a touch of elegance and grandeur.
